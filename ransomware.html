Ransomware Encryption

Imagine arriving at your desk, coffee in hand, only to find your screen displaying a terrifying countdown clock and a demand for 10 Bitcoin. This is the reality of a ransomware attack.

How It Happens

  • Infiltration: Often starts with a simple phishing link or a vulnerability in a remote desktop protocol.
  • Propagation: Once inside, the malware silently spreads through the network, identifying sensitive databases and backups.
  • Execution: At a coordinated moment, the malware encrypts all discovered files using strong, military-grade cryptography.

Critical Rule: Never pay the ransom. It funds criminal organizations and there is no guarantee you will get your data back. Focus on offline backups and restoration.
